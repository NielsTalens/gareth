## Extracted Product Vision

#### Target Groups *(Confidence: High)*
- Sales reps who dislike CRM admin work
- Founders doing their own sales (especially early stage)
- Potentially customer success people later (future expansion)

#### Needs *(Confidence: High)*
- Reduce CRM “admin work” (clicking, updating fields, dashboards)
- Provide clear, prioritized “what to do next” guidance
- Minimize fragmentation across tools (CRM/email/calendar/Slack)
- Eliminate manual data entry (logging, pipeline updates)
- Enable fast execution of follow-ups and next steps
- Emotional needs: feel in control, not overwhelmed; reduce stress/guilt from being “behind”

#### Product Features *(Confidence: High)*
- Conversation-first CRM (chat interface as primary UI; possible voice later)
- “Next best action” suggestions (ideally proactive), prioritized list of actions
- Context attached to actions (who + context + suggested message/next step)
- Quick execution actions from chat (send follow-up, call, schedule)
- Automatic activity capture/logging (emails, calls; notes generated/suggested)
- Background automation: pipeline updates without manual input
- Minimal/no traditional screens; avoid dashboards/reporting upfront; reduce choices/options

#### Business Goals *(Confidence: Medium–High)*
- Reduce CRM fatigue and increase daily adoption/usage
- Increase revenue per rep (via better follow-up execution)
- Make onboarding/training minimal or unnecessary

#### Differentiators *(Confidence: High)*
- “Action > insights”: focuses on telling users what to do rather than charts/analytics
- Conversation-first with minimal/no traditional CRM screens/tabs/dashboards
- Automation-by-default; “zero data entry” expectation
- Not a reporting/BI tool; not endlessly configurable/customizable like Salesforce
- Product feel/tone: focused, direct, calm, decisive (not gamified/cluttered)

## Evidence

- **Target Groups:**
  - “sales reps who hate CRM admin”
  - “founders doing their own sales (especially early stage)”
  - “maybe customer success people later”

- **Needs:**
  - “CRM as it exists today… is kind of broken… It’s too much clicking around, updating fields, checking dashboards… feels like admin work”
  - “What we want instead is something that just… tells you what to do next.”
  - “they want to know what to do next, clearly”
  - “they don’t want to manually update anything”
  - “today they usually check like 3–5 things (CRM, email, calendar, maybe Slack) → already fragmented”
  - “users don’t just want efficiency… to feel in control… not feel overwhelmed… not feel like they’re doing admin work… CRM = stress + guilt”
  - “→ remove searching → remove updating → remove deciding”

- **Product Features:**
  - “It’s basically a CRM but conversation-first. Probably chat based, maybe voice later.”
  - “tell you your next best action”
  - “log stuff automatically (calls, emails etc.)”
  - “keep the pipeline updated without you touching it”
  - “let you execute things quickly (send follow-up, schedule, etc.)”
  - “system suggests actions before you ask (ideally)”
  - “each item includes: who, context, suggested message or next step”
  - “notes generated or at least suggested”
  - “everything updates in background, invisible”
  - “reduce choices, don’t give users 10 options”
  - “We’re intentionally not doing: traditional screens… analytics dashboards (at least not upfront)”

- **Business Goals:**
  - “reduce the general fatigue people have with CRM”
  - “get reps to actually use the system daily”
  - “increase revenue per rep (if they follow up more, this should happen)”
  - “make onboarding basically nonexistent or very fast… If someone needs training, we probably failed.”

- **Differentiators:**
  - “conversation first, always… everything through chat / language, no screens if we can avoid it”
  - “actions > insights… avoid anything that looks like reporting”
  - “automation by default… if user has to fill fields → something went wrong”
  - “not a reporting tool… not BI… not a place to input data… not something you customize endlessly… basically not another version of Salesforce”
  - “Everything is more like: → action → next step → done. Less ‘viewing’, more ‘doing’.”

## Suggestion
- Add **measurable business outcomes** (e.g., target % increase in daily active usage, time saved per rep/day, follow-up SLA improvements, revenue lift assumptions).
- Clarify **initial go-to-market boundary** (e.g., SMB vs mid-market, outbound vs inbound teams, required integrations like Gmail/Outlook + calendar).
- State explicit **core promise** in one sentence (e.g., “A CRM that proactively tells you the next action and executes/logs it automatically via chat.”).
- Define a small set of **non-goals/guardrails** as hard constraints (e.g., “no dashboards in v1,” “no custom fields/workflows in v1”) to preserve differentiation.
