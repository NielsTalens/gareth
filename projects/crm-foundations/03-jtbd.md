### 1) Extracted JTBD

#### Job Name
Get the next best sales action without CRM admin

**Confidence:** High

#### Core Job Statement
When I start my day (or check my pipeline), I want the system to tell me what to do next and let me execute it immediately, so I can keep deals moving without spending time on CRM maintenance.

**Confidence:** High

#### Desired Outcomes
- Clear, prioritized next actions (ranked so “no thinking required”)
- Fast execution of follow-ups (send/schedule/call) from the same interface
- Automatic capture of emails/calls + suggested/generated notes
- Pipeline stays updated “in the background” with “basically zero data entry”
- Fewer missed follow-ups (“often forget something”; “fall through cracks”)
- Feel less fatigued; increase daily CRM usage; faster onboarding (as business goals, but tied to user outcomes)

**Confidence:** High

#### Emotional or Social Dimension
- Feel “in control of [the] pipeline”
- Not feel “overwhelmed”
- Avoid “stress + guilt” from an always-behind CRM
- Ideal state: “calm, clear, just doing the next thing”

**Confidence:** High

#### Current Frictions
- Too much clicking, switching, and manual updating (“admin work”)
- Fragmented workflow across CRM/email/calendar/Slack
- Manual deciding what to do next; unclear priorities
- Manual logging and stage updates get skipped because it’s “too much effort”
- Dashboards require interpretation and still don’t translate into action

**Confidence:** High

#### Existing Alternatives or Workarounds
- Traditional CRMs: “Salesforce / HubSpot”
- “Pipedrive (simpler but still manual)”
- “spreadsheets”
- “notes / reminders / calendar hacks”
- Checking multiple tools in the morning (CRM, email, calendar, Slack)

**Confidence:** High

#### User Flows or Journeys
- Morning/opening laptop → user’s first question: “what do I do first”
- Current “figuring out next action” vs desired (open CRM → click/search/decide vs open system → ask/get prioritized actions with context)
- Executing an action: current (find record → read notes → switch to email → write → maybe log → update stage) vs desired (chat command/click → action executes → auto-logging + pipeline updates)
- Capturing activity: current (log notes, inconsistent auto-capture, manual pipeline updates) vs desired (automatic capture, suggested notes, stage updated without asking)
- Staying on top of deals: current (dashboards, interpret, still unclear) vs desired (flags convert to executable actions)

**Confidence:** High

## Suggestion
- Add 1–2 concrete personas + trigger contexts (e.g., “AE with 40 open deals”, “founder doing 5 demos/week”) to sharpen the job scope.
- Define success metrics tied to outcomes (e.g., “time-to-next-action”, “% activities auto-logged”, “follow-up SLA adherence”) to make the JTBD more testable.
- Capture explicit “hiring criteria” vs “deal-breakers” (e.g., must integrate with Gmail/Outlook; must not require manual stage changes) since “no screens” and “no dashboards” are principles but not yet framed as user acceptance criteria.
